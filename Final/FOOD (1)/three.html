ATKORI:

Aktori is an ancient recipe from the dry valleys of Lahaul-Spiti in Himachal Pradesh. This delectable sweet is very easy and quick to make and is typically prepared during festivities and celebratory event. The buckwheat leaves are mixed with the flour of wheat and then prepared in pancakes. Top the dish with honey and ghee or simply serve the dish with hot with chutney.It can be served for breakfast or as a tidbit.

INGREDIENTS:-

1. Buckwheat Flour - 1 cup
2. Wheat Flour - 1 cup
3. Milk - 1/2 cup
4. Water - 1/2 cup
5. Baking Soda - 1 tsp
6. Sugar - 4 tbsp
7. Oil/Ghee to fry

COOKING STEPS:-

• In a bowl mix together wheat flour and buckwheat flour.
• To this add, equal amounts of milk and water and whisk it into a batter having thick consistency
• Add sugar as per the taste. After that add baking soda and mix well. Keep the batter aside for 15 minutes
• In a pan heat ghee/oil over medium flame. When heated enough, add a ladle full of this batter and spread it evenly.
• Cook this on low flame. When you start seeing bubbles and the above surface becomes less liquidy, flip over.
• After it is cookes on the other side also, serve it on a plate
• Voila!!!The AKTORI is ready .Serve with honey/chutney.
